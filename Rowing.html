Rowing for the First Time is Easy for Everyone

Rowing is a diverse sport, offering different types of rowing opportunities, both on and off water. Fun events have grown in popularity since the growth of rowing in Britain. Many clubs host a scratch regatta, where rowers are drawn in random crews to race each other and take part in multiple rounds, over a short distance.

All Types of People

Everyone is welcome into the sport, whatever the ambition is. Whether the aspiration is to become an olympian or someone who fancies trying something different every one in a while. It may be because they want to meet new people and make friends or be more adventurous and try the thrill of competing.

Teenagers

11 to 18 year olds who want to learn to row in either fixed or sliding seat boats are given support to learn skills.
They are also provided the opportunity of an environment to make friends and share a fun experience together.

Adults

Adults who want to learn to row, in either fixed or sliding seat boats, will learn to row with other novices in 'the same boat'.
They will get the opportunity to try new skills and spend lots of time on the water. The 'Learn to Row' award is designed to provide a structured route into sports for adults.

Disabled Individuals

Anyone can get involved in rowing, regardless of physical disability, sensory or learning impairment.
Adaptive rowing is accessible to so many disabilities. There always seems to be a solution to each individual's issues, allowing them to get on a boat, and onto the water, whatever their disability.
